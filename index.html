Factors affecting Indigenous Educational Prospects

When analysing the Australian educational landscape, it is important to understand the factors that contribute to socio-educational disadvantage in indigenous students. Barriers affecting indigenous education can be understood through the lens of the ICSEA score, a ranking given to schools which gives an indication of parents occupation and education, as well as the location of the school.

We took a look at how students in Australia are performing...

Non-indigenous students are more likely to pass than their indigenous peers...

So we dove deeper... How does remoteness affect our school children?

Despite both groups of students being in the same very remote location, the attainment of non indigenous students remains relatively stable, whereas there is a sharp drop off in the achievement of indigneous students.

So why is remoteness dramatically more damaging to Indigenous students?


One way we can understand this is by considering their ICSEA score. This is a ranking given to a school which takes into consideration the education and occupation of their student's parents. The score of 1000 is considered average, and either end of the spectrum gives us a unique insight into how advantaged or disadvanged the typical is.
